Sound Visuals

Many people including myself have focused on visualizing sound and music.
I was very curious about the emotional background of sounds and what could be changed to emphasize their experience.

I started by visualizing amplitude and frequency in simple shapes.
So I created my first sound visualizing program to get the technical basics. I used Processing because I found it to be the quickest way of creating prototypes. I tried to stick to the bases of a laptop and didnâ€™t use any external microphones.
I quickly realized that only simple shapes may not be the way to go. It created an already known diagram-like picture and my intend was to create a different experience.
So I moved on to a more complex looking picture and I liked the look of three dimensional waves to create depth.
My next move was to set up a program to fully influence different factors and live-test their effect on the experience. I still sticked to three dimensional waves on that one.
I had two main takeaways from that: three dimensional images create an interesting depth but waves leave not a lot space for own impressions and color can severely change the mood in which music is seen.

So my final approach was not to overcomplicate the picture but still get a three dimensional feel and the possibility to adjust colors for different mood settings.
This is still on my mind and from time to time I touch back on the subject.
